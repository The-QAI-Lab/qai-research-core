# Paper Outliner Skill — Behavioral Validation Tests

- Is the outline complete, logically structured, and standards-compliant?
- Are all sections relevant, justified, and suitable for the research topic and target journal/format?
- Are claims explicitly linked to evidence in each section?
- Are missing evidence and weak sections surfaced and flagged?
- Is a figure/table strategy present and actionable?
- Is the Reviewer Exposure Block present and machine-readable?
- Is the publication readiness checkpoint explicit?
- Is the output structured with explicit section headers and section notes?
- Does the skill avoid generic, incomplete, or non-actionable outlines?
- Are domain-specific needs (e.g., harmonization, sensor details) addressed?
- Is the output suitable for direct use by Reviewer Mode skill?
- Does the skill request clarification if study results or target format are ambiguous?
- Does it handle errors and missing data gracefully, reporting them explicitly?
